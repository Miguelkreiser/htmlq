The government cannot be trusted to prioritize First Nations issues. The Chiefs of the reserves cannot be trusted to handle the government's funding properly. Safe clean drinkable water is a human right. It is unconstitutional that First Nations people need to move off reserves to urban areas to access clean water. Ignoring Firs Nations water quality issues devalues them as human beings. As a developed country conditions of First Nations water quality are shameful. First Nations people shouldn’t be given preferential treatment over other communities living with water quality issues. A densely populated city would never have a 20 year boil advisory. The First Nations people have sufficient access to potable water. First Nations people don’t have the training/expertise to maintain the water treatment plants. Simply chlorinating the water is not a sustainable solution. We shouldn’t stand idly and watch this injustice remain unchallenged. I personally do not see an issue with drinking bottled water. Having to walk for long periods of time for drinkable water is emotionally and physically draining. There are greater benefits for First Nations people by managing their own water quality affairs. Governments of all levels need to provide support to all the reservations to make amends for past injustices. The mismanagement of resources by the First NAtions communities is the reason why their water quality is poor. It's not up to us to dictate to the Government of Canada how to solve water quality issues for First Nations people. The Canadian government should be cooperative when working with First NAtions people. First Nations people are counting on taxpayer money to fix the issue for them. The governemnt should prioritize our funds towards the third world water conditions found on First Nations reserves. The money used to ship in bottled water should be used to create the needed infrastructure.
